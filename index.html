About Us

Tau Beta Pi is universally recognized as the preeminent engineering honor society that students of all engineering disciplines strive to join.

Tau Beta Pi is the nation's second oldest honor society, founded at Lehigh University in 1885. It is the only engineering honor society that recognizes individuals from all engineering disciplines. There are currently 241 chapters at colleges and universities across the U.S., 32 active alumni chapters across 15 districts, and a total initiated membership of over half a million. Tau Beta Pi's vision is to recognize, celebrate, and champion excellence in engineering.

The New York Nu chapter at the University at Buffalo, established in 1967, has inducted 3,767 members as of the Fall of 2023. Buffalo's chapter NY Nu organizes a wide variety of events for club members and the campus community. Most notable and long lived are our Honors Dinner and engineering tutoring programs,

Since it's inauguration 485,337 members have been inducted into Tau Beta Pi. Currently there are 230 active collegiate chapter in the United States. For more information on the national organization please visit www.tbp.org
